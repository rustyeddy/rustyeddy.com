Controlling a Water Pump with a Transistor

One important part of the garden-station (an IoT project I've been workiing on) is controlling pump with a signal from the Raspberry Pi (or other MCU). Rather than use a relay I built a circuit using a transistor, I'll discuss how it's done.

The project was created to use a Soil Moisture Sensor to drive a water pump to keep our plants watered.

Bill Of Materials

  1. 1 - Inexpensive 5v submersible pump
  2. 1 - 1K Ohm Resistor
  3. 1 - mps2222a Transistor
  4. 1 - 1N4001 Diode
  5. 1 - Raspberry Pi (model of your choosing, zero’s work great)
  6. 1 - External 5v Battery Supply (don’t want to power the pump with the PI)
