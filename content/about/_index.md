## In the Beginning
 
My interest in computer programming started with the Commodore 64 while I
was attending L.A. Harbor Community College, taking classes to become a
Mechanical Engineer.
 
Soon after I transferred to CSU Long Beach I switched majors to Computer
Science, and just prior to graduating I took a job in aerospace.
 
## The Golden Age of Aerospace
 
Employed as a Member of the Technical Staff at *Rockwell Intl., Space
Systems Division*, I began managing a network of *Sun Microsystems*
“engineering workstations,” as they were called at the time.
 
Soon after that I began programming embedded systems and real-time
applications. While at Rockwell I started attending *USC* for my master's
degree, where I focused on Computer Networks and Distributed Systems.
 
## Institutionalized at USC
 
I left Rockwell to join a short-lived startup called *Retix Systems*, where
I led a QA lab for routing protocols. From there I was offered a position at
the *Information Sciences Institute* at *USC*, where I worked on multicast
routing research and developed one of the earliest implementations of the
*PIM* multicast routing protocol.
 
## The Dotcom Craze and Cisco Systems
 
That led to a five-year run as a Technical Lead at *Cisco Systems* on a
brand-new product called the *Core Routing System (CRS)*. At the time the
hardware was still being whiteboarded and we wrote the source code from
scratch.
 
I was involved in the early development of MSDP, PIM, IGMP, multicast
forwarding, and other interesting things. After the project moved from R&D
into production, it was time to move on.
 
## Out on My Own
 
From Cisco I started my own consulting company, *Eddy Consulting*, where I
have had the chance to work with many diverse and interesting companies,
projects, and people across a wide range of technologies.
 
Even after all of those projects, Computer Networks and Embedded Systems
remain my favorites — which is exactly where my work has trended ever since.
 
## IoT, Go, and the Physical World
 
Networking taught me how machines talk to each other. Embedded systems
taught me how software meets the physical world. IoT is where those two
interests finally collide.
 
These days I build and teach practical IoT, Linux, and Go automation
systems — small computers that observe and alter the real, physical world,
and talk to each other while doing it. My focus is on treating IoT as a
serious software engineering problem: clean architecture, well-defined APIs,
reliable messaging, and systems that scale, evolve, and stay maintainable
long after the first device ships.
 
Projects like **OttO** (an IoT edge gateway written in Go) and **RedEye**
grew out of that approach, alongside the essays and notes you'll find
throughout this site.
 
## What I'm Doing Now
 
I am currently a Member of the Technical Staff at *Panasonic Avionics*,
working on connected systems at scale.
 
I still program almost every day. I've held titles like *Principal
Engineer*, *Technical Lead*, and *Senior Systems Programmer*, but I am most
content when I'm actually writing code or automating a system. I do my best
work on new, not-yet-well-defined problems — turning complex concepts and
good ideas into real, working software.
 
I am especially drawn to projects with a purpose: environmental cleanup,
industrial automation, and using small, inexpensive hardware to solve real
problems.
